Demonstration – Validity Dates and Multiple Commission Recipients

This demonstration provides an overview of how to use validity periods for commission rates and how to use multiple commission recipients on one customer with the identification of the primary recipient.

Scenario: Kevin, the sales manager at CRONUS, decides to increase the sales volume within the LONDON responsibility center for a specific item. He grants Mark, who is responsible for that area, higher commission rates for a short period to push this project.

In addition, he grants Adam Green, who was the responsible commission recipient for the customers in Great Britain serviced from the LONDON responsibility center, an extra commission for these customers.

Susan, the sales order processor, enters a new sales order after Mark visits a customer and receives a new order.

At the end of the month, Arnie, the accounts receivable administrator, creates the commission statements, verifies several values and then issues the commission statement for Mark and Adam.

Specify Commission Rates for Validity Periods

Phyllis, the accounting manager, enters Marks’ commission rates for item 1964-W, INNSBRUCK Storage Unit. The commission rate is only valid for sales orders posted from February 11th to 15th, 2019.

Follow these steps to enter commission rates:

  1. On the cc|commission menu, click Commission Rates.
  2. In the header, set the following filters:
    • Sales Type Filter = Customer Commission Group
    • Comm. Recipient Type Filter = Commission Recipient
    • Comm. Recipient Code Filter = C-0005
    • Type Filter = Item
    • Code Filter = 1964-W
  3. On the first line, enter the following information:
    • Sales Code = RETAIL
    • Commission Type = Percent
    • Commission = 6
    • Starting Date = 02/11/19
    • Ending Date = 02/15/19
  4. On the next line, enter the following information:
    • Sales Code = STANDARD
    • Commission Type = Percent
    • Commission = 6
    • Starting Date = 02/11/19
    • Ending Date = 02/15/19

: Commission Rates for Mark Colins for item 1964-W

  1. Click OK to close the Commission Rates page.

Allocate Multiple Commission Recipients to One Customer

Phyllis re-allocates Adam Green to the customers in Great Britain serviced from the LONDON responsibility center. Adam receives 25% from the normal commission calculated for sales documents of these customers.

Follow these steps to allocate multiple commission recipients to customers:

  1. On the Sales & Marketing menu, click Sales > Customers.
  2. Locate and select customer 50000, Guildford Water Department.
  3. On the ribbon, click Navigate > Commission Recipients.
  4. On the next line, in the Commission Recipient No. field, enter C-0002 (Adam Green).
  5. In the Share % field, type 25.

: Commission Recipients for customer 50000

  1. Click OK to close the Customer Comm. Recipient Catalog page.
  2. Locate and select customer 60000, Blanemark Hifi Shop.
  3. Repeat steps 3–6.
  4. Locate and select customer 61000, Fairway Sound.
  5. Repeat steps 3–6.
  6. Locate and select customer 62000, The Device Shop.
  7. Repeat steps 3–6.

Create Posted Sales Document Lines for Items

Susan, the sales order processor, enters a new sales order after Mark visited his customer 60000, Blanemark Hifi Shop and made a new order on February 7, 2019. Since the order can be delivered directly, Susan can post the shipment and the invoice.

On February 14, 2019, Susan enters the next new sales order after Mark has visited his customer 61000 Fairway Sound and made a new order. The order can be delivered directly. Susan can post the shipment and the invoice.

Follow these steps to enter and post two sales orders:

  1. On the Sales & Marketing menu, click Order Processing > Sales Orders.
  2. On the ribbon, click New and then press ENTER.
  3. In the Sell-to Customer No. field, enter 60000.
  4. In the Posting Date field, enter 02/07/19.
  5. In the Order Date field, enter 02/07/19.
  6. On ribbon, click Navigate > Commission Recipients to confirm that the correct commission recipients are allocated to the sales order.

: Commission Recipients assigned to the sales order

  1. Close the Sales Commission Recipients page.
  2. On the first sales line, select Item in the Type field.
  3. In the No. field, enter 1964-W.
  4. In the Location Code field, enter BLUE.
  5. In the Quantity field, type 5.
  6. On the ribbon, click Home > Post.
  7. With the Ship and Invoice option selected, click OK.
  8. Click No to close the confirmation message without opening the posted invoice.
  9. On the ribbon, click New and then press ENTER.
  10. In the Sell-to Customer No. field, enter 61000.
  11. In the Posting Date field, enter 02/14/19.
  12. In the Order Date field, enter 02/14/19.
  13. Review the allocated Commission Recipients as needed.
  14. On the first sales line, select Item in the Type field.
  15. In the No. field, enter 1964-W.
  16. In the Location Code field, enter BLUE.
  17. In the Quantity field, type 3.
  18. On the ribbon, click Home > Post.
  19. With the Ship and Invoice option selected, click OK.
  20. Click No to close the confirmation message without opening the posted invoice.

Automatically Create Commission Statements

At the end of the month, Arnie, the accounts receivable administrator, creates the monthly commission statements for Mark and Adam.

Follow these steps to create an automated commission statement:

  1. On the cc|commission menu, click Create Commission Statements.
  2. In the Posting Date field, enter 02/28/19.
  3. In the Document Date field, enter 02/28/19.
  4. In the Statement from field, enter 02/01/19.
  5. In the Statement to field, enter 02/28/19.
  6. On the Commission Recipient FastTab, enter C-0002|C-0005 in the No. field.

: Create Commission Statement for both Adam and Mark

  1. Click OK to run the Create Commission Statement batch job.

Verify Commission Lines

After he has created the commission statement for Mark and Adam, Arnie checks the commission statement and verifies the values.

To check the commission statement and verifying the values:

  1. On the cc|commission menu, click Commission Statements.
  2. Locate and open the commission statement for Adam Green.

: Commission Statement for Adam

  1. On the Lines FastTab, click Line > Calculation Information. Confirm the information, for example, that the calculation only uses 25% of the commission amount to calculate the commission.

: Commission line information for Adam

Note

The commission percentage is adjusted to reflect the 25% share requirement.

  1. Close the Commission Line Information and Commission Statement pages.
  2. Locate and open the commission statement for Mark Colins.

: Commission Statement for Mark showing different commissions based on posting dates

Notice that the lines have different commission values due to the date on which the sales documents were posted. This is not the case for Adam (refer to Fig. 4), who did not have validity dates assigned for the sale of this item.

  1. On the Lines FastTab, click Line > Calculation Information. Confirm the information, for example, that the calculation uses 100% of the commission amount to calculate the commission.

: Commission line information for Mark

  1. Close the Commission Line Information and Commission Statement pages.

Issue Commission Statements

After Arnie checks the commission statements, he issues and then prints the commission statement for Mark and Adam to use it as the basis for the payment.

Follow these steps to issue and print issued commission statements:

  1. On the ribbon of the Commission Statements list page, click Issue.
  2. On the Commission Statement Header FastTab, enter CS-10005|CS-10006 in the No. field (the commission statements for Adam and Mark).

: Issue Commission Statement for Adam and Mark

  1. Click OK to run the Issue Commission Statement batch job.
  2. On the cc|commission menu, click Issued Commission Statements.
  3. Select the last issued commission statement for Adam (ICS-10002) and then click Print.

: Printing Commission Statement for Adam

  1. Click Preview to review the commission statement for Adam.

: Printed Commission Statement for Adam

  1. Close the Print Preview page.
  2. Repeat steps 5–7 to review the commission statement (ICS-10003) for Mark.

: Printed Commission Statement for Mark

  1. Close the Print Preview page.
