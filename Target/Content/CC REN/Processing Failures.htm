Processing Failures

Failures are processed based on the following parameters:

  • Periods within the invoicing period are regarded as negative lines (credit lines) on the invoice.
  • Periods before the current invoicing period are regarded in a separate Credit Memo.
  • Periods after the current invoicing period are regarded as negative lines (credit lines) on the invoice in the proper period.

These scenarios are demonstrated in the following sections.

Exercise – Failure Period within the Invoicing Period

Customer R-30000 orders four units of item R-30000-01 for the period of January 1, 2019 through March 3, 2019 at a price of 350.00 each. This is a monthly invoicing agreement; however the customer has asked to be invoiced for January and February immediately.

At the beginning of March, the customer calls to say that one of the units is damaged. They want to return it and not be billed for it for the month of March.

Create, Ship and Invoice the Rent Order

Follow these steps to create the rent order:

  1. Set the Work Date to 01/01/19.
  2. In the navigation pane, click cc|rental > Contract Management > Rent Orders.
  3. Insert a new rent order for customer R-30000.
  4. On the first line, enter the following:
    1. Type = Item
    2. No. = R-30000-01
    3. Quantity = 4
    4. Rental Rate Code = Month
    5. Month Based = Yes
    6. Rental Start Date = 01/01/19
    7. Rental End Date = 03/31/19
  5. On the ribbon, click Post.
  6. With the Ship option selected, click OK.
  7. On the ribbon, click Post.
  8. Select the Invoice option and then click OK.
  9. In the Posting Date field, enter 01/01/19.
  10. In the Invoice to Date field, enter 02/28/19.
  11. Click OK to create the invoice and then accept all confirmation messages.
  12. On the ribbon, click Navigate > Invoices.
  13. Open the invoice and then on the ribbon, click Post and then click Yes to post the invoice.
  14. Escape back to the rent order.

Create the Failure

Follow these steps to create the failure:

  1. On the Lines FastTab, click Line > Enter Failure.
  2. Click Yes to create the failure period.
  3. In the Start Date Failure field, enter 03/01/19.
  4. In the End Date Failure field, enter 03/31/19.
  5. In the Failure Type field, enter DAMAGE.
  6. In the Quantity field, enter 1.
  7. On the ribbon, click Release.

: Released Rental Failure Card

  1. Click OK.
  2. Click the value in the Qty. Failures field and review the failure information.

: Failure assigned to the rental line

  1. Escape back to the rent order.

Process the Invoice with the Failure

Follow these steps to process the invoice with the failure:

  1. On the ribbon, click Home > Post.
  2. Select the Invoice option and then click OK.
  3. In the Posting Date field, enter 03/01/19.
  4. In the Invoice to Date field, enter 03/31/19.
  5. Click OK to create the invoice and then accept all confirmation messages.
  6. On the ribbon, click Navigate > Invoices.
  7. Open the invoice and notice the following:
    • A negative credit line for the failed unit from 03/01/19 – 03/31/19 appears on the invoice.
    • The credit is correctly calculated for the damaged unit (350 rate unit price for the month of March).

: Failure assigned to the rental line

  1. On the ribbon, click Post and then click Yes to post the invoice.
  2. Escape back to the Rent Order.
  3. On the Navigate tab, click Entries > Rental Ledger Entries.
  4. Notice that the credit line appears for the proper amount, in the proper date range, and for the proper quantity.

: Rental Ledger Entries including failure credit

  1. Escape back to the rent order.
  2. Click the value in the Qty. Failures field.
  3. In the Rental Failures page, notice that the failure invoice was created through 03/31/19.

: Updated rental failure line

  1. Escape back to the rent order.

Process the Return

Follow these steps to process the return:

  1. In the Posting Date field, enter 03/05/19.
  2. On the Lines FastTab, enter 03/05/19 in the Rental Return Date field.
  3. In the Return Qty. to Receive field, type 1.
  4. On the ribbon, click Home > Post.
  5. Select the Receive option and then click OK.
  6. Notice that the Return Qty. Received and Quantity Invoiced fields are 1.
  7. Process the return of the remaining three items on 03/31/19.
  8. Notice that the Return Qty. Received and Quantity Invoiced fields are 4.

Exercise – Failure Period before the Invoicing Period

Customer R-30000 orders five units of item R-30000-01 for the period of January 1, 2019 through March 31, 2019 at a price of 350 each. This is a monthly invoicing agreement and the January invoice is posted immediately.

In mid-January, the customer calls to say that two of the units are damaged. You assure them that the items can be fixed at no cost for the down period of January 15th through January 20th and tell them that they will receive a credit memo with the next invoice billing.

Create, Ship, and Invoice the Rent Order

Follow these steps to create the rent order:

  1. Insert a new rent order for customer R-30000.
  2. On the first line, enter the following:
    1. Type = Item
    2. No. = R-30000-01
    3. Quantity = 5
    4. Rental Rate Code = Month
    5. Month Based = Yes
    6. Prepaid = Yes
    7. Rental Start Date = 01/01/19
    8. Rental End Date = 03/31/19
  3. On the ribbon, click Post.
  4. With the Ship option selected, click OK.
  5. On the ribbon, click Post.
  6. Click the Invoice option and then click OK.
  7. In the Posting Date field, enter 01/01/19.
  8. In the Invoice to Date field, enter 01/31/19.
  9. Click OK to create the invoice and then accept all confirmation messages.
  10. On the ribbon, click Navigate > Invoices.
  11. Open the invoice and then on the ribbon, click Post and then click Yes to post the invoice.
  12. Escape back to the rent order.

Create the Failure

Follow these steps to create the failure:

  1. On the Lines FastTab, click Line > Enter Failure.
  2. Click Yes to create the failure period.
  3. In the Start Date Failure field, enter 01/15/19.
  4. In the End Date Failure field, enter 01/20/19.
  5. In the Failure Type field, enter DAMAGE.
  6. In the Quantity field, enter 2.
  7. On the ribbon, click Release.
  8. Click OK.
  9. Click the value in the Qty. Failures field and review the failure information.
  10. Escape back to the rent order.

Process the Invoice with the Failure

Follow these steps to process the invoice with the failure:

  1. On the ribbon, click Home > Post.
  2. Click the Invoice option and then click OK.
  3. In the Posting Date field, enter 02/01/19.
  4. In the Invoice to Date field, enter 02/28/19.
  5. Click OK to create the invoice and then accept all confirmation messages, noting that one invoice and one credit memo was created.

: Invoice and Credit Memo creation message

  1. On the ribbon, click Navigate > Invoices.
  2. Open the invoice and notice that no credit entries appear.

: No failure lines assigned to invoice

  1. On the ribbon, click Post and then click Yes to post the invoice.
  2. Escape back to the rent order.
  3. On the Navigate tab, click Credit Memos.
  4. Open the credit memo and notice that the credit is correctly calculated by the number of days for the two units (350 rate unit price / 31 days * 6 days * 2 units = 135.48).

: Failure credit memo

  1. On the ribbon, click Post and then click Yes to post the credit memo.
  2. Escape back to the rent order.
  3. On the Navigate tab, click Entries > Rental Ledger Entries.
  4. Notice that there are two credit memo entries for the proper amount, in the proper date range.

: Rental Ledger Entries including failure credit

  1. Escape back to the rent order.
  2. Click the value in the Qty. Failures field and notice that the failure invoice/credit memo was created through 01/20/19.

: Updated rental failure line

  1. Escape back to the rent order.

Process the Final Invoice and Return

Follow these steps to process the final invoice and complete the return:

  1. On the ribbon, click Home > Post.
  2. Click the Invoice option and then click OK.
  3. In the Posting Date field, enter 03/01/19.
  4. In the Invoice to Date field, enter 03/31/19.
  5. Click OK to create the invoice and then accept all confirmation messages.
  6. On the ribbon, click Navigate > Invoices.
  7. Open the invoice and then on the ribbon, click Post and then click Yes to post the invoice.
  8. Escape back to the rent order.
  9. In the Posting Date field, enter 03/31/19.
  10. On the Lines FastTab, enter 03/31/19 in the Rental Return Date field.
  11. In the Return Qty. to Receive field, enter 5.
  12. On the ribbon, click Home > Post.
  13. Select the Receive option and then click OK.
  14. Notice that the Return Qty. Received field is 5.

Exercise – Failure Period after the Invoicing Period

Customer R-30000 orders five units of item R-30000-01 for the period of January 1, 2019 through March 31, 2019 at a price of 350 each. This is a monthly invoicing agreement and January will be posted immediately.

Before other invoicing occurs, you notify the customer that an inspection is required on two items which may cause some downtime from March 1st through March 5th so you will credit 70% of the price for this time on the March invoice. The customer agrees to this arrangement and you create the failure.

Create, Ship, and Invoice the Rent Order

Follow these steps to process the rent order:

  1. Insert a new rent order for customer R-30000.
  2. On the first line, enter the following:
    1. Type = Item
    2. No. = R-30000-01
    3. Quantity = 5
    4. Rental Rate Code = Month
    5. Month Based = Yes
    6. Rental Start Date = 01/01/19
    7. Rental End Date = 03/31/19
  3. On the ribbon, click Post.
  4. With the Ship option selected, click OK.
  5. On the ribbon, click Post.
  6. Click the Invoice option and then click OK.
  7. In the Posting Date field, enter 01/01/19.
  8. In the Invoice to Date field, enter 01/31/19.
  9. Click OK to create the invoice and then accept all confirmation messages.
  10. On the ribbon, click Navigate > Invoices.
  11. Open the invoice and then on the ribbon, click Post and then click Yes to post the invoice.
  12. Escape back to the rent order.

Create the Failure

Follow these steps to create the failure:

  1. On the Lines FastTab, click Line > Enter Failure.
  2. Click Yes to create the failure period.
  3. In the Start Date Failure field, enter 03/01/19.
  4. In the End Date Failure field, enter 03/05/19.
  5. In the Failure Type field, enter INSPECTION. Notice that the Discount % is set to 70.
  6. In the Quantity field, enter 2.

  8. Click OK.
  9. Click the value in the Qty. Failures field and review the failure information.

  1. Escape back to the rent order.
