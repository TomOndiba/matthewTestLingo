Exercise – Sell a Rented Truck to a Customer

On February 3, 2019 customer Tanner Transport (R-20000) rents a truck for approximately two months because the company is short of transportation vehicles at that time.

When the customer receives the March 3rd invoice, they realize that they will be in need of the truck for a much longer period of time and the one that is rented meets all their needs. The customer asks to buy the truck at the end of the rental period as a used one instead of renting it any longer.

Create a Rent Order

Follow these steps to create a rent order:

  1. Insert a new rent order for customer R-20000.
  2. Expand the Rental FastTab and enter the following:
    1. Rental Rate Code = Month
    2. Rental Start Date = 02/03/19
    3. Rental End Date = 03/31/19
    4. Prepaid = Yes
  3. On the first line of the Lines FastTab, enter the following:
    1. Type = Item
    2. No. = R-20000-01
    3. Quantity = 1
    4. Rate Unit Price = add if values are missing on Item or Rate Component lines
  4. On the Lines FastTab, click Line > Item Tracking Lines.
  5. With the Shipment option selected, click OK.
  6. On the ribbon, click Select Entries.
  7. Note which serial number is selected and then click OK.
  8. Click OK to close the Item Tracking Lines page.
  9. On the ribbon, click Post.
  10. With the Ship option selected, click OK.
  11. On the ribbon, click Post.
  12. Select the Invoice option and then click OK.
  13. In the Posting Date field, enter 02/03/19.
  14. In the Invoice to Date field, enter 03/02/19.
  15. Click OK and accept all confirmation messages.
  16. Open and post the Service Invoice Rental.
  17. Set the Work Date to 03/03/19.
  18. On the ribbon of the Rent Order, click Home > Post.
  19. Select the Invoice option and then click OK.
  20. In the Posting Date field, enter 03/03/19.
  21. In the Invoice to Date field, enter 04/02/19.
  22. Click OK and accept all confirmation messages.
  23. Open and post the Service Invoice Rental.

Sell the Rented Truck

Follow these steps to sell the rented truck from the Rent Order:

  1. In the Posting Date field, enter 04/02/19.
  2. In the Rental Return Date field on the rental item line, enter 04/02/19.
  3. In the Return Qty. to Receive field, enter 1.
  4. On the ribbon, click Home > Reopen.
  5. Delete any unposted One-Time Invoicing positions for services to be performed on returned items, for example, a charge to clean the rented item is no longer applicable because the item will not be physically returned to the rental company.
  6. Use the “Choose Columns” function to add the Sell Item from Rent field to the Lines FastTab.
  7. Select the Sell Item from Rent check box on the rental item line.
  8. On the Lines FastTab, click Functions > Sales Allocation.

: Sales from Rental page

  1. Select the Use Charge check box.
  2. In the Sales Price field, enter the sales price of the truck.
  3. Click the Chargeable Rental Profit amount.

: Rental Entry Select page

  1. Click Actions > Charge All to select the Charge to Sales Price check box on each line.

Note

The Charge to Sales Price check box can be selected/cleared manually on each line as well.

  1. Click OK. Notice that the Sales Amount (per Unit) and Sales Amount (Qty.) fields now contain the Sales Price minus the amounts selected for charge.

: Sale from Rental page with chargeable amounts applied

  1. Repeat steps 9–13 for Chargeable Rate Part Profit and Chargeable One Time Profit as needed.
  2. Click OK to close the Sale from Rental page.
  3. On the Lines FastTab, click Line > Item Tracking Lines.
  4. Select the Receive option and then click OK.
  5. Confirm that the serial number is correct and then click OK to close the Item Tracking Lines page.
  6. On the ribbon, click Post.
  7. Select the Receive option and then click OK. Notice the following:
    • The receipt is processed on the original item line (and any relevant rate components).
    • A new Sales line is added that contains the calculated Sales Price as the Unit Price.

: Rent Order after rental item sales processing

  1. Select the Sales line and then on the Lines FastTab, click Line > Item Tracking Lines.
  2. On the ribbon, click Select Entries.
  3. Confirm that the serial number originally rented is selected and then click OK.
  4. Click OK to close the Item Tracking Lines page.
  5. On the ribbon, click Post.
  6. With the Ship option selected, click OK. The following message appears:

: Customer No. change confirmation request

  1. Click Yes to change the Customer No. on the service item from the Internal Customer to the customer that is purchasing the item.
  2. On the ribbon, click Post.
  3. Select the Invoice option and then click OK.
  4. In the Posting Date field, enter 04/02/19.
  5. In the Invoice to Date field, enter 04/02/19.
  6. Click OK to create the invoice and then accept all confirmation messages.
  7. On the ribbon, click Navigate > Invoices.
  8. Open the invoice and notice that item R-20000-01 appears on the line with the calculated Sales Allocation amount as the unit price.

: Sales invoice for the sold item

  1. On the ribbon, click Post and then click Yes to post the invoice.
  2. Escape back to the rent order.

This exercise showed how to process the sale of rented item that occurred when the rental period ended. Depending on when/how the return is processed, there are different sales invoicing situations:

  • If rent revenues were invoiced past the Rental Return Date, the system will create a Credit Memo on invoicing.
  • If invoicing has not yet reached the Rental Return Date, the system will add a sales position on the next Invoice (which then can be included with the Sales Invoicing).
  • If the return was already posted, manually create a Sales Order from the Rent Order by clicking Actions > Create Sales Order on the ribbon.
