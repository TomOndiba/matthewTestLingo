Solve Internal Service Costs Batch Job

cc|rental offers a batch job that locates the amounts posted to a customer account and reallocates these amounts to the G/L account specified in the Internal Service Cost Account field on the General Posting Setup page (refer to topic "Rental Revenue Accounts" on page 1 for more information).

: General Posting Setup, highlighting Internal Service Cost Account

In the navigation pane, click Service > Order Processing > Solve Internal Service Costs to open the batch job.

: Solve Internal Service Cost batch job page

In the Options FastTab, enter the Posting Date and indicate which journal to send the entries.

In the Customer FastTab, filter on customers with open Customer Ledger Entries that can be balanced against the internal Cost Account and prepared for posting by creating General Journal lines.

The appropriate Internal Cost Account is found using the General Product Posting Group from the Service Item entered on the Service Item Line and the General Business Posting Group from the customer.

For example, an internal service order is created to customer R-99999 for item R-10000-01, with a maintenance expense (275.00) and an item cost (22.50). The posted service entries are shown in Fig. 3 below. The item cost is posted to the sales account (plus VAT) and the maintenance expense is posted to the expense account (plus VAT). The total cost is posted to the customer’s accounts receivable account.

: Posted G/L entries for an internal service order

The “Solve Internal Service Cost” batch job is run for the customer and then the selected journal is populated with the entries to be balanced upon posting. As part of this process, entries are made that balance the receivables account, because a payment entry is not otherwise created for an internal customer.

: Journal entries created from the Solve Internal Service Cost batch job
