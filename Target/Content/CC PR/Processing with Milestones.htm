Processing with Milestones

Milestones can be established within jobs to aid in evaluating the status and timeliness of a project. Milestones do not have durations or costs associated with them.

Milestones can be used as a trigger for each sales channel.

: Job Sales Codes with and without milestone settings

Milestones might be included in the calculation structure for the following reasons:

  • A milestone triggers the calculation for a fixed price.
  • Reaching multiple milestones will release the cost for the invoice run.

Fig. 2 shows an example of how to build the calculation structure with milestones.

: Job Budget Calculation structure with and without milestone settings

In the Job Budget Calculation view, the milestones (levels) are set up on the main position in the Job Sales Milestone Triggers page (Navigate > Milestone Triggers). For example, to add the milestones (of positions 2.3–2.5) for WBS position 2., select position 2. and then open the Job Sales Milestone Triggers page.

: Milestones assigned to job position 2.

In the Job Budget Sales view, the Sales Milestone field is used to make users aware of a referenced milestone. The milestones can also be edited and defined in the Job Sales Milestone Triggers page by clicking the value in the Sales Milestone field.

: Job Sales Milestone Triggers page, opened from the WBS position 1. in the Job Budget Sales view

The confirmation of milestones automatically starts the planning for an invoice run for the relevant sales line. Milestone completion can be confirmed by selecting the Milestone Completed check box on the Job Planning and Processing view.

: Milestone Completed check box selected on line in the Job Planning and Processing view

Milestone confirmation can also be managed in the Job Milestones page, accessed from the Job Card by clicking Navigate > Milestones.

: Milestone Completed check box selected on lines in the Job Milestones page
