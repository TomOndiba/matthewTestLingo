Basic Principles of Project Management

This section describes the basic principles of project management.

Project Definition

In general a project is a finite endeavor (having specific start and completion dates) undertaken to create a unique product or service which brings about beneficial change or added value. In this case, a project is a plan which is characterized in its entirety by the uniqueness of the conditions, for example, scope, time frame, financial budget, personnel and other limitations, and job-specific organization.

Jobs are:

  • Limited in time and scope
  • Separate/novel/unique
  • Complex/coordination-intensive
  • Uncertain/hard to plan

Job management includes:

  • Systematic planning
  • Organization
  • Controlling and monitoring

Goals:

  • Quality of service
  • Adherence to delivery dates
  • Compliance with cost and profit budget
