Job Liquidity Integration

The integration of the standard Microsoft Dynamics NAV Liquidity module (Cash Flow) into cc|project allows for an in-depth analysis of cash flow for job-related sales and purchases. This topic describes functionality that is specific to cc|project. For detailed information about setting up or using the standard Job Liquidity module, refer to Online Help relating to Cash Flow.

Setup

Settings to use Cash Flow in cc|project are defined in the Cash Flow Setup and Jobs Setup pages. Each is described in this section.

Cash Flow Setup

The Cash Flow Setup page – accessed from the navigation pane by clicking Financial Management > Cash Flow > Cash Flow Setup – contains two fields to identify the cash flow accounts for Job Cost and Job Sales.

: Cash Flow Setup with Job Cost and Job Sales account fields highlighted

Field

Description

Job Cost CF Account No.

Specifies the number of the cash flow account in which the values for job costs should be recorded. Open job procurement entries and/or scheduled job liquidity entries are used to calculate job costs.

Job Sales CF Account No.

Specifies the number of the cash flow account in which the values for job sales should be recorded. Scheduled job liquidity entries are used to calculate job sales.

 

Jobs Setup

On the Planning FastTab of the Jobs Setup page, the Cash Flow Pmt. Code field indicates the payment terms for the cash flow date calculations.

: Cash Flow Payment Code field on Jobs Setup

Job Planning

This section describes the usage of the Job Liquidity integration in the Job Planning process.

Job Planning and Processing with Cash Flow

Multiple fields in the Job Planning and Processing view display information about the cash flow for the job.

: Job Liquidity fields on Job Planning and Processing view

The fields relating to Job Liquidity in the Job Planning and Processing view are described in the following table.

Field

Description

TDC Liquidity Schedule (LCY)

Indicates the total direct cost that has been scheduled, in local currency; used for the Cash Flow calculation.

TDC Liquidity Request (LCY)

Indicates the total direct cost that has been requested, in local currency.

TDC Liquidity Payment (LCY)

Indicates the total direct cost that has been paid, in local currency.

TP Liquidity Schedule (LCY)

Indicates the total price that has been scheduled, in local currency; used for the Cash Flow calculation.

TP Liquidity Request (LCY)

Indicates the total price that has been requested, in local currency.

TP Liquidity Payment (LCY)

Indicates the total price that has been paid, in local currency.

The values in the fields are determined by setting in the Liquidity Type field in the Liquidity Entries page, accessed by clicking Navigate > Liquidity Entries.

: Scheduled Job Liquidity Entries

As illustrated in Fig. 4, 3000.00 has been scheduled for total direct cost and 5000.00 has been scheduled for sales. These values are visible in the TDC Liquidity Schedule (LCY) and TP Liquidity Schedule (LCY) fields in the Job Planning and Processing view.

The fields in the Job Liquidity Entries page are described in the following table.

Field

Description

Liquidity Type

Indicates the type of liquidity entry. The options are:

  • Schedule – used for the Cash Flow calculation.
  • Request – currently has no functionality.
  • Payment – currently has no functionality.

Date

Indicates the date on which this liquidity entry is valid.

Description

Short description of the liquidity entry.

Special Position

Currently has no functionality.

Total Direct Cost (LCY)

Specifies the total direct cost for the liquidity entry, in local currency; used for the Cash Flow calculation.

Total Cost (LCY)

Specifies the total cost for the liquidity entry, in local currency.

Sales Line Amount (LCY)

Specifies the sales line amount for the liquidity entry, in local currency; used for the Cash Flow calculation.

Person Responsible

Specifies a resource that is responsible for the liquidity entry.

Job No. / WBS

Transfers from the Job Planning and Processing line.

Job Procurement No.

Transfers from the Job Procurement line.

 

Procurement with Cash Flow

Once Cash Flow has been set up in the Job Planning and Processing view, any procurement will result in an update of the Liquidity Entries. This periodic update must be done manually if needed.

: Procurement information on the Job Processing Procurement view

Cash Flow Worksheet

Once all job processing has been completed, the entries can be managed in the Cash Flow Worksheet. The Options FastTab on the Suggest Worksheet Lines request page includes settings for cc|project.

: Suggest Worksheet Lines request page in Cash Flow Worksheet

The options relating to cc|project are described in the following table.

Option

Description

Procurement Material

Select to include procurement materials in the cash flow forecast.

Procurement Service

Select to include procurement services in the cash flow forecast.

Liquidity Entries Purchase

Select to include purchase liquidity entries in the cash flow forecast.

Liquidity Entries Sales

Select to include sales liquidity entries in the cash flow forecast.

The Cash Flow Worksheet is populated with all relevant entries when the “Suggest Worksheet Lines” function is run.

: Cash Flow Worksheet entries suggested for all cc|project options as well as Receivables and Payables

Once the lines are registered, all entries can be viewed from the Cash Flow Forecast Card. Additionally, an overview of all entries, including Job Procurement, Job Liquidity Entries Purchase and Job Liquidity Entries Sales, is available from the Cash Flow Forecast Statistics page.

: Job-related entries in the Cash Flow Forecast Statistics

Job-related information is also available in the CF Availability by Periods page, accessed from the Navigate tab on the Cash Flow Forecast Card.

: Monthly job-related cash flow information on CF Availability by Periods
