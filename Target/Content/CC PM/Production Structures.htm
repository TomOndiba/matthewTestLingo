Production Structures

When a production structure is created for an item with ingredients, the related ingredients are copied to the Prod. Structure Ingredients page, accessed on the ribbon by clicking Navigate > Ingredients.

: Ingredients for item P-60017 copied to the production structure header

Some of the fields in the Prod. Structure Ingredients page are described in the following table. 

Field

Description

Quantity (Expected)

Displays the Item Ingredients that were assigned to the manufacturing item.

Alloc. Unit of Measure Code

Defines the relationship between ingredient quantity and component quantity.

The value can be edited to convert the quantity when producing in different amounts.

Quantity (Calc.)

Displays the sum of the Quantity (Calc.) fields of all component ingredients per ingredient.

This field is set to zero (0) until component lines are added to the Production Structure lines (refer to topic “Production Structure Component Ingredients” on page 1).

Production Structure Component Ingredients

A component or variant with ingredients can be entered in the Lines FastTab of the Production Structure as an Item or Share of Recipe type.

Ingredients are accessed from the Lines FastTab by clicking Line > Ingredients. The ingredients can be modified per production structure. Normally, modifications of the ingredients should be performed directly on the (component) item card and should only differ per production structure in exceptional cases.

Note

Ingredients can be added to lines without an item (Type = Item and No. = <blank>) on Development Structures and Laboratory Structures. However, to certify the Laboratory Structure the lines must have an item number. When the “New Item” function is run from the Production Structure line, the ingredient information will be transferred as Item Ingredients for the new item.

To reach the specified ingredient quantity of the item to be produced, quantities must be allocated to the components in the Quantity per or Share % fields.

: Components with ingredients added to Production Structure lines

The system automatically calculates the ingredient quantity in the Prod. Structure Line Ingredients page based on the specified ingredient quantity of the item. As illustrated in Fig. 3, the Quantity (Calc.) field is calculated for ingredients of component items P-60018 and P-60019 by multiplying the Quantity per of the production structure line by the Quantity of the ingredient.

: Calculated ingredients for component items P-60018 and P-60019

Recipe ingredients are calculated based on the Share % in relation to the recipe weight of the item in the Production Structure header.

Important

Ingredients must be calculated in quantities – ingredient potency cannot be defined in percentages.

Expected and Calculated Ingredient Quantities

In the Quantity (Calc.) field in the Prod. Structure Ingredients page of the item to be produced, the system automatically displays the sum of the Quantity (Calc.) fields of all component ingredients per ingredient. Thus, users can control within this page if the specified potency of the item to be produced can be achieved with the allocated component ingredients.

Ingredients added to the components will appear as new lines in the Prod. Structure Ingredients page with the Quantity (Expected) field set to 0.

: Expected and calculated ingredients assigned to a production structure

As illustrated in Fig. 4, the expected and calculated ingredient quantities do not match because of the following calculated component quantities:

  • Ingredient No. INH-0001
    • Component P-60018, 4 mg
    • Component P-60019, 9 mg
  • Ingredient No. INH-0002
    • Component P-60018, 8 mg
    • Component P-60019, 15 mg

Note

Expected and calculated quantities can be different. If the production structure is certified without equal quantities, the expected quantity will be overwritten with the calculated ingredient quantities. However, these changes are not transferred back to the item.

When the Production Structure is certified, the header and line ingredients are copied to the Production BOM header and lines and will be used with production orders.

: Expected and calculated ingredient quantities assigned to the production BOM header, and base/calculated ingredient quantities assigned to the production BOM lines

Note

The Production BOM Header and Line Ingredients cannot be changed or deleted. Any changes that need to be made to the ingredients in a BOM/Recipe must be done in the corresponding Production Structure.

Exercise – Create and Certify Production Structure

Create a Production Structure for item P-I10, Product and add component lines with the following share of recipe:

  • Item A = 10%
  • Item B = 20%
  • Filler = 70%

Confirm that all ingredients are calculated properly. Change the Allocation Unit of Measure to ensure that the quantities are correctly recalculated. Certify the Production Structure and review the Production BOM header and line ingredients.

Create Production Structure

Follow these steps to create the production structure:

  1. In the navigation pane, click Manufacturing > Product Design > Production Structures.
  2. On the ribbon, click New and then press ENTER to automatically populate the No. field.
  3. In the Item No. field, enter P-I10.
  4. In the Lot Size field, type 100.
  5. On the ribbon, click Navigate > Ingredients.

: Ingredients for item P-I10 copied to the production structure header

  1. Click OK to close the Prod. Structure Ingredients page.
  2. On the first line, enter Share of Recipe in the Type field.
  3. In the No. field, enter P-I01, Item A.
  4. In the Share % field, type 10.
  5. On the Lines Fast Tab, click Line > Ingredients.

: Ingredients from Production Structure line

  1. Note that the Quantity (Calc.) = 1 MG, based on the ingredient calculation of Share % x Item Ingredient Quantity for Ingredient 1 (10% x 10 = 1).
  2. Click OK to close the Prod. Structure Line Ingredients page.
  3. Go to the next line and enter Share of Recipe in the Type field.
  4. In the No. field, enter P-I02, Item B.
  5. In the Share % field, type 20.
  6. On the Lines Fast Tab, click Line > Ingredients.

: Ingredients from Production Structure line

  1. Note that the Quantity (Calc.) = 6 MG, based on the ingredient calculation of Share % x Item Ingredient Quantity for Ingredient 1 (20% x 30 = 6).
  2. Click OK to close the Prod. Structure Line Ingredients page.
  3. On the Navigate tab, click Ingredients.

: Calculated ingredient quantities from Production Structure lines

  1. Note that the Quantity (Calc.) is 1 for Ingredient 1 (from the first component) and 6 for Ingredient 2 (from the second component).
  2. Click OK to close the Prod. Structure Ingredients page.
  3. Go to the next line and enter Share of Recipe in the Type field.
  4. In the No. field, enter P-I07, Filler.
  5. In the Share % field, type 70.

Change Allocation Unit of Measure

The value in the Alloc. Unit of Measure field defines the relationship between Ingredient Qty. and Component Qty. Therefore, Ingredient 1 = 10 MG per KG of the component, and Ingredient 2 = 30 MG per KG of the component.

The Allocation Unit of Measure field on the manufacturing item is the reference unit that is needed to calculate the absolute quantity. Therefore, to show the quantity in one unit of measure to another, e.g. 10 G per KG, units of measure per Allocation Unit of Measure is needed.   

Follow these steps to change the allocation unit of measure to confirm recalculation:

  1. On the Navigate tab, click Ingredients.
  2. In the line for Ingredient 1, change the Alloc. Unit of Measure from KG to GR.

: Preparing to change the allocation unit of measure

  1. Note that the Quantity (Calc.) has changed to .001.

: Recalculated quantity based on changing the allocation unit of measure

  1. Reset the Alloc. Unit of Measure Code field back to KG.
  2. Do not close the Prod. Structure Ingredients page.

Certify the Production Structure

Follow these steps to certify the Production Structure:

  1. On the Prod. Structure Ingredients page, note that the Quantity (Expected) and Quantity (Calc.) do not match.

: Expected and calculated quantities do not match

  1. On the General FastTab, change the Status to Certified.
    A warning message appears stating that the expected ingredient quantities will be overwritten with the calculated ingredient quantities.

: Warning message on certification because expected and calculated quantities do not match

  1. Click Yes to continue with the certification process.
  2. On the Navigate tab, click Ingredients.
  3. Note that the expected and calculated quantities now match.

: Expected and calculated quantities match after certification

  1. Click OK to close the Prod. Structure Ingredients page.
  2. In the Production Structure Details FactBox, click the item number link for P-I10 to open the Item Card.
  3. On the ribbon, click Actions > Ingredients.
  4. Note that the item ingredient quantities were not changed to match the calculated quantities from the production structure certification.

: Item Ingredients quantities remain unchanged after production structure certification

  1. Click OK to close the Item Ingredients page and then close the Item Card.

Review Recipe Ingredients

Follow these steps to review the ingredients on the recipe:

  1. On the General FastTab on the Production Structure, click the recipe link in the Production BOM No. field and then click Advanced.
  2. In the Production BOM/Recipe List page, click Navigate > Card.
  3. In the Recipe Header page, click Navigate > Ingredients.
  4. Note that the expected and calculated quantities were transferred from the production structure.

: Expected and calculated ingredient quantities assigned to the production BOM header

  1. Click Close to close the Production BOM Header Ingredients page.
  2. Select the first line and then click Component > Ingredients.

: Expected and calculated ingredient quantities assigned to the first production BOM line

  1. Click Close to close the Production BOM Line Ingredients page.
  2. Select the second line and then click Component > Ingredients.

: Expected and calculated ingredient quantities assigned to the second production BOM line

  1. Close all the open pages.
